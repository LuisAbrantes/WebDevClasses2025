Harvard University

Founded in 1636, Harvard is the oldest institution of higher learning in the United States. It is renowned for its law, business, and medical schools.

Yale University

Established in 1701, Yale is known for its strong arts and drama programs. Its library is one of the largest in the world.

Princeton University

Founded in 1746, Princeton is famous for its focus on undergraduate education and its strong programs in mathematics and physics.

Columbia University

Located in New York City, Columbia is known for its journalism and international affairs programs. It has strong ties to the city.

University of Pennsylvania

Founded by Benjamin Franklin, Penn is known for its Wharton School of Business and its strong programs in medicine and engineering.

Dartmouth College

Located in Hanover, New Hampshire, Dartmouth is known for its focus on undergraduate education and its beautiful rural setting.

Brown University

Established in 1764, Brown is known for its open curriculum, which allows students to design their own course of study.

Cornell University

Founded in 1865, Cornell is a land-grant university known for its strong programs in engineering, agriculture, and hotel administration.
